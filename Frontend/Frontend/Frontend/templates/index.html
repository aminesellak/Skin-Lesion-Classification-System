Melanocytic nevi - Usually noncancerous, these type of lesions ard commonly called birth marks or moles. Can rarely become cancerous, consult your doctor if new or growing


Melanoma - Skin cancer, these types of lesions grow very quickly. Please consult your doctor.


Benign keratosis-like lesions - Noncancerous, these skin lesions develop as people age. Very common.


Basal cell carcinoma - Cancerous, the skin lesions often develop on areas exposed by the sun. Generally not harmful, but consult your doctor.


Actinic keratoses - Usually noncancerous, these types of skin lesiosn occur normally due to old age and sun exposure. Due to risk of becoming cancerous, consult your doctor.


Vascular lesions - Rarely cancerous, these lesions are very common and are normally referred to as birthmarks. Consult your doctor if growth occurs.



Dermatofibroma - Noncancerous, these generally occur in women and are harmless. Consult your doctor if concerned.
